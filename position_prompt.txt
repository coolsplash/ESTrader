THESIS-BASED EXIT (ACTION = "close")
A trade MUST be closed when the thesis is objectively invalid, defined by multiple confirming conditions:
1. Loss of the critical setup-defining level
The reclaim / sweep / retest / breakout level that justified the entry is lost AND:
•	Price breaks through decisively,
•	AND fails to reclaim it,
•	AND order flow supports the opposite direction (persistent takers, not just a wick).
2. True structure reversal
Not just a pullback — an actual reversal:
•	For longs: clear sequence of lower highs AND lower lows with sustained selling.
•	For shorts: clear sequence of higher highs AND higher lows with sustained buying.
•	Temporary counter-rotations do NOT qualify.
3. Sustained opposite-direction aggression
Defined as:
•	Multiple rotations of opposing takers dominating,
•	OR persistent delta pressure against the position,
•	OR clearly shifting imbalance in the opposite direction.
4. Liquidity + absorption flipping against the trade
•	For longs: formerly supportive liquidity becomes resistance and price reacts to it.
•	For shorts: formerly capping liquidity becomes support and price reacts constructively.
•	Must be meaningful, not a momentary test.
5. Stop would need to be widened
You MUST NOT widen stops.
If the correct structural stop is now beyond the original invalidation zone → the thesis is invalid → close.
Healthy pullbacks, retests, shallow traps, or temporary delta drops DO NOT invalidate the trade.
